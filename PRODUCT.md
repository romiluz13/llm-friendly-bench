# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MongoDB sellers, customer engineering leaders, skeptical developers, architects, AI platform owners, and finance stakeholders evaluating the cost of building ordinary application software with coding agents.

## Product Purpose

AST-Bench proves how database shape affects AI coding-agent work. The plain-language promise is: show how much extra reading, guessing, fixing, and reviewing an AI agent has to do before the same feature ships. Raw prompts, traces, diffs, tests, database proof, screenshots, cost assumptions, and promotion gates must remain available, but they are the audit trail, not the first experience. Success means a MongoDB seller, CFO, CTO, and skeptical Postgres-oriented developer can understand the point in under one minute, then drill into proof without finding hidden claims.

## Brand Personality

Forensic, direct, technically serious.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing pages, internal seller enablement language, decorative dashboards, hidden caveats, fake live demos, unearned benchmark claims, and any copy that sounds like guaranteed savings.

## Design Principles

- Show the shipped workflow before discussing cost.
- Translate every machine artifact into human language before showing raw logs.
- Make every click move evidence, state, or the receipt.
- Keep mixed metrics visible, even when MongoDB loses one.
- Fail closed when verified evidence is missing.
- Treat benchmark status as a product state, not fine print.
- Compare one MongoDB data plane against realistic multi-system stacks, such as Postgres plus search plus vector database, only when the benchmark lane has real captured evidence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use strong focus states, readable dense evidence, keyboard-operable controls, no color-only meaning, and reduced-motion support for all animation.
